The Technology

From sunlight and CO₂ to permanent carbon storage.

Our photobioreactor systems are modular, park-integrated infrastructure — engineered for urban deployment and verified carbon outcomes.

How It Works

Four steps. Closed loop.

Step 01

Capture

Algae inside sealed tubular reactors absorb CO₂ directly from urban air. Each unit processes ambient air continuously, no point-source required.

Step 02

Grow

Photosynthesis converts CO₂ into dense algal biomass. Our reactor geometry maximises light exposure and minimises land footprint.

Step 03

Convert

Harvested biomass undergoes hydrothermal carbonisation on-site, producing hydrochar — a stable, carbon-rich solid.

Step 04

Store

Hydrochar is buried or used in soil amendment. Carbon remains locked for an estimated 100–300 years, fully verifiable.

System Architecture

Three subsystems. One closed loop.

Algae cultivation, mechanical processing, and hydrothermal carbonisation are co-located within each installation. Harvested biomass moves directly from growth panels through concentration and filtration into the HTC reactor — producing solid hydrochar on-site with no transport required. Process water is recycled back into the growth system.

Algae Growth System GFRC-shell panels with external air intake. CO₂ absorbed directly from ambient urban air — no point source required.
Mechanical Core Slurry concentration centrifuge and filtration press. ~1,000L slurry processed per 500L batch, weekly harvest cycle, ~4 hours processing time.
HTC Reactor 500L batch reactor at 200°C, 50 bar. Exothermic startup with insulated heating coils. Output: ~30 kg dry hydrochar per batch.
