The Solution

One closed loop,
six steps.

Step 01
Intake
Ambient CO₂-rich air is drawn through an external intake louver into the photobioreactor panels.
Step 02
Grow
Algae fix CO₂ photosynthetically, accumulating biomass. Airlift mixing and sensors maintain optimal conditions.
Step 03
Harvest
Algae-rich culture is centrifuged weekly, separating dense biomass from the water stream. Process water is recycled back into the system.
Step 04
Convert
Biomass enters the compact on-site HTC enclosure. The hydrothermal carbonization unit converts wet algae to hydrochar at 200°C, 50 Bar — no drying needed.
Step 05
Store
Hydrochar — a stable, carbon-rich solid — accumulates in the collection vault. Collected quarterly for verification and credit issuance.
Step 06
Verify
Each batch of hydrochar is independently verified. Real-time sensor data — CO₂, air quality, temperature, humidity — provides a full audit trail for carbon credit issuance.

Why 0asis

Built differently,
by design.

Durable carbon storage
Hydrochar is stable across centuries to millennia. Unlike tree planting or soil carbon, it cannot be reversed by fire, drought, or land use change. Storage is physical and verifiable.
Park-first deployment
Designed to integrate with existing parks and public spaces — not remote industrial sites. Each unit is modular, low-footprint, and engineered for public environments.
Biologically driven cost
Algae are the engine. No exotic chemistry, no massive energy input. Capture cost falls as biology scales — a fundamentally different cost trajectory than Direct Air Capture.
Real-time, auditable monitoring
Integrated sensors track CO₂ concentration, air quality, temperature, and humidity continuously. Every credit is backed by a sensor-verified, time-stamped data record.

Field Proof

Belgrade. Where the
concept was proven.

In 2021, LIQUID3 demonstrated that photobioreactor technology works in a real polluted city — surviving variable temperatures, urban pollution loading, and public deployment conditions in Belgrade, Serbia. That proof of concept established the biological foundation 0ASIS builds on.

Organisation LIQUID3
Year 2021
Location Belgrade, Serbia
System type Photobioreactor · Public deployment
What it proved Biology works in real urban conditions
What it left open Permanent carbon storage
F·01
Photobioreactor technology survived real urban conditions — variable temperatures, pollution loading, and public deployment in Belgrade — confirming the biology transfers from lab to city.
F·02
What LIQUID3 left open was the carbon loop. Harvested biomass decomposed and released its carbon back — the system cleaned the air but didn't lock anything away.
F·03
0ASIS closes that loop. By adding on-site hydrothermal carbonization, harvested biomass is converted into permanent hydrochar rather than released — transforming a working air quality system into a verified carbon removal system.
